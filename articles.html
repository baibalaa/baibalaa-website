Match Day 3 Delivers Intensity, Rivalries, and Strong Statements

Published on: 24th March 2026, 01:35

Match Day 3 of the Baibalaa tournament was successfully held on Monday, 23rd March 2026, featuring six exciting encounters that kept fans engaged throughout the evening. With strong performances, revenge battles, and dominant displays, the competition continues to heat up as teams push for qualification.

Match 18 featured the second teams of two well-known clubs — KUDA HENVEIRU STREET and THE BEEGEES (BG). In this competitive clash, THE BEEGEES emerged victorious with a 2–0 win, showcasing depth and promising talent within their squad structure.

Last year’s finalists, LORENZO SPORTS CLUB, continued their impressive form this season. Facing LT SPORTS CLUB, Lorenzo demonstrated experience and composure to secure another 2–0 victory. The previous year’s finalists are clearly building momentum and once again proving they are strong contenders for the title.

Match 20: BG SPORTS CLUB vs CLUB KAASINJEE – A Thriller Decided in Gadha Hifun

One of the most anticipated fixtures of the night lived up to every expectation. The rivalry between BG SPORTS CLUB and CLUB KAASINJEE added extra intensity, especially after their dramatic Quarter Final meeting last year where Club Kaasinjee triumphed.

This was undoubtedly the match of the night.

In Match 21, KAASINJAAS delivered a strong performance against MEET THE EAGLES. Controlling the game effectively, Kaasinjaas secured a confident 2–0 victory, keeping their campaign on track.

Match 22: THE WOLVES CLUB 2 – 0 HULHUMALE SPORTS CLUB

The final match of the night saw tournament's one of the favourites THE WOLVES CLUB face HULHUMALE SPORTS CLUB. Living up to expectations, THE WOLVES CLUB dominated proceedings and secured a convincing 2–0 win. As one of the tournament’s favourites, they continue to show why many consider them serious title challengers.

A Competitive Night of Baibalaa

The King Leaves the Arena — A Tribute to Mohamed Shinan “Naani” 👑

Published on: 19 March 2026, 15:58

The arena will never feel the same again.

In a sport that has witnessed countless warriors, moments of brilliance, and unforgettable battles, there are only a few who rise above it all — not just as players, but as legends. And among them stands one name that echoes louder than the rest: Mohamed Shinan “Naani.”

Today, the King of Baibalaa leaves the arena. Baibalaa has seen many great players over the years. But Naani was different. He wasn’t just great — he was complete. Widely regarded as the best all-rounder the game has ever seen, he dominated both ends of the arena. In attack, he was fast, fearless, and relentless. In defence, he was a wall — aggressive, commanding, and unbreakable.

He was not a player teams could prepare for. He was a player teams feared. There were moments when his presence alone changed the game. Attacking players hesitated. Strategies shifted. Confidence dropped. That is the kind of impact only a true king can have — when the game begins to revolve around you.

And then there are the records — not just numbers, but milestones that shaped Baibalaa history. Naani became the first player to be crowned Mr Baibalaa three times, each time with a different team — a feat that speaks volumes about his adaptability, leadership, and unmatched influence. - 1421 — Maziya Sports: Champion & Mr Baibalaa - 1428 — Buru Sports Club: Champion & Mr Baibalaa - 1431 — BG Sports Club: Champion & Mr Baibalaa Three different teams. Three different journeys. One constant — Naani at the top.

A champion with three clubs, a seven-time champion overall, and one of the most consistent players in Baibalaa history, his career was built not just on talent, but on something even rarer — discipline.

For over two decades, he gave everything to the game. And in a sport where longevity itself is a challenge, Naani didn’t just last — he remained at the very top.

His final chapter only adds to the legend. In the 1445 tournament, representing Buru Sports Club, Naani once again stood tall — not as a fading legend, but as a champion. While many players step away when their time has passed, he chose a different path. He left the arena at the peak, as a champion, with dignity and pride. A beginning that was great. An ending that was even greater. Not every player gets that story. His journey feels almost unreal — like a fairytale written in sand, sweat, and gold. A career every player dreams of, but only a few could ever achieve.

But beyond the titles, beyond the victories, beyond the fear he instilled in opponents — Naani’s legacy is something deeper. It is in his discipline. His mentality. His respect for the game. The standard he set for generations to come.

As he steps away from the arena, Naani leaves not just memories, but heartfelt gratitude. He thanks everyone who stood by him throughout his journey — the clubs that became part of his story: Maziya Sports, BG Sports Club, Buru Sports Club, Club Teenage, United Henveirans, and TC Sports Club. He extends his appreciation to their management and the fans who supported him through every victory and every challenge.

And to the next generation, the King leaves behind a message: Believe in yourself. Discipline is the power behind success. And Baibalaa is a truly outstanding sport — respect it, play it, and carry it forward.

And that is why his name will never fade. Long after the matches are over, long after new champions rise, one thing will remain unchanged — the echo of his name in the Baibalaa arena. The throne may now stand empty. But the legacy of the King will live on.

Thank you, Naani. 👑

🏆 Honouring Our Legends: Why Baibalaa Deserves a Lifetime Achievement Award

Published on: 15 November 2025, 12:01

Every year, when the drums echo across the Baibalaa grounds and the crowd roars in excitement, the Eid Baibalaa Challenge reminds us why this sport is so special. It’s not just about who wins or who scores the fastest point — it’s about the spirit, the brotherhood, and the love for a game that has lived in our islands for generations.

But as we celebrate the rising stars and thrilling finals, I often find myself thinking — what about those who built this game? The ones who carried it on their shoulders long before social media, trophies, or lights. The ones whose sweat, time, and love made Baibalaa what it is today.

Shouldn’t we, as a community, pause for a moment each year to say thank you?

🌟 The Unseen Heroes of Baibalaa

Baibalaa has always been more than a sport. It’s culture. It’s community. It’s a story written in sand — shaped by hundreds of players, coaches, and officials who gave everything to keep the game alive.

There are legends who never stopped showing up. Coaches who built champions quietly. Officials who stood in the heat year after year. Players who taught discipline, unity, and respect — and inspired countless kids to step into the ring.

Their names might not always make headlines, but their contribution is the reason Baibalaa still stands strong today.

🕊️ A Simple Idea: Recognize and Remember

What if, every year during the Eid Baibalaa Challenge, we honored one of these individuals — a player, a coach, an official, or even a supporter — who has given a lifetime of love to the sport?

Just as film awards celebrate a “Lifetime Achievement,” Baibalaa could celebrate its legends with a Baibalaa Lifetime Achievement Award or Spirit of Baibalaa Award.

It doesn’t need to be grand or glittering. A simple moment, a simple recognition — but one filled with meaning. One that says:

“We see you. We remember you. You are part of this game’s history.”

🔥 What It Would Mean

Such recognition would not just honor one person. It would honor every person who has ever carried the torch for Baibalaa. It would remind the young players that this sport isn’t only about speed and strength — it’s about legacy.

It would show that we, as a community, value passion and dedication just as much as trophies and titles.

Because when we remember our past, we strengthen our future.

🙏 A Call to the Baibalaa Association

To the Baibalaa Association of Maldives — this is my humble suggestion, from the heart of a fan and a believer: Let’s create this moment every year. Let’s give the stage once, not to a winner, but to a builder.

Let the crowd stand and clap for a name that made us all fall in love with this sport. Let’s give Baibalaa what it truly deserves — a way to honor its legends.

❤️ In the End. Years from now, when future players step onto the sand, they’ll remember the champions — but they’ll also remember the heroes who made it possible.

And maybe that’s the greatest victory of all — to be remembered not for the points you scored, but for the hearts you touched, the lessons you taught, and the game you helped build.

Baibalaa: A Sport We Can Proudly Call Our Own

Published on: 28th April 2025, 22:35

Baibalaa is a sport deeply rooted in our heritage — a game we can truly call our own. It holds a special place in the hearts of Maldivians, with a rich history and a passionate following across the country. We should take pride in this unique sport, not only as a symbol of our culture but as something with the potential to reach far beyond our shores.

One of the main reasons behind launching this website was to introduce Baibalaa to a wider audience. I am happy to share that we have already seen some success in this mission. The website now has active users from countries such as the USA, Malaysia, India, Saudi Arabia, Sri Lanka, Singapore, Thailand, and France. This growing international interest shows that there is potential for Baibalaa beyond our borders, and it encourages us to continue efforts to promote the sport internationally..

In my opinion, while we look to expand the sport, it is equally important to preserve its prestige locally. Currently, we have the Baibalaa Championship organized once every year during Eid by the Baibalaa Association of Maldives. I firmly believe that this championship should remain exclusive and be held only once a year. Much like the World Cup, the anticipation and rarity of the event add to its significance and excitement. Hosting it more frequently might dilute its impact and prestige.

However, to keep the spirit of Baibalaa alive year-round, there is room for other tournaments. These can include office tournaments, regional tournaments, or club tournaments — similar to the successful office tournament held in 2023. These events can be organized by other parties or organizations, who can affiliate with the Baibalaa Association of Maldives for support and collaboration in hosting them., while the main championship remains the pinnacle event organized solely by the Association once a year.

The future of Baibalaa is bright. With the right balance between preserving tradition and expanding our horizons, we can ensure that Baibalaa not only thrives in the Maldives but also finds its place on the global sporting stage.

This is my vision — one that I am passionate about — and I fully understand that others may have different opinions. But one thing is clear: Baibalaa is something we all can be proud of, and together, we can take it to new heights.

TC Sports Club – A Legacy of Consistency, Passion, and Historic Impact

Published on: 10th April 2025, 23:58

In the rich history of Baibalaa, few names have echoed with as much consistency and resilience as Trust and Care Sports Club, better known as TC Sports Club. From the earliest days of the sport, TC Sports Club has remained a powerful presence, always among the top contenders, always fighting with heart.

In the early years, TC Sports Club often found themselves reaching the Quarter Final stage—always close, always strong, but unable to break through. That changed in 1427 (2006), when TC Sports made a statement. They stunned the tournament by defeating one of the top favourites, Maziya Sports Club, a team filled with legends like Ibrahim Shinaan "Naani" and Athiko. This was the turning point.

That year, TC Sports made it to the Semi Final for the first time. Although they narrowly lost in Gadha Hifun to BG New Generation, they still took home glory with Yameen Ibrahim "Yaambe" being crowned Mr Baibalaa of that edition—a moment of pride that marked TC’s rise.

From there, TC Sports entered their golden era, becoming regular semi-finalists and reaching their peak between 2011 and 2013, when they made three consecutive finals. In 1432 (2011), they finally lifted the trophy, being crowned champions of the tournament. The following two years, they remained just a step away, finishing as runners-up in both 2012 and 2013.

But TC Sports is not just a Baibalaa powerhouse—they are a true multi-sport club. As their success in Baibalaa grew, they also turned their focus to football. In 2014, TC Sports earned promotion to the First Division, and what followed was nothing short of remarkable. They won the Dhivehi Premier League, and went on to become the first Maldivian club to win an international trophy by lifting the Sheikh Kamal International Cup.

Despite their football success, TC Sports never left Baibalaa behind. They continued to reach the quarter-finals and semi-finals, proving their strength year after year. In Eid Baibalaa Challenge 1446, they once again made it to the quarter-final stage, only to be stopped by the eventual champions, T.F.T. And their legacy goes even deeper. This year’s champion team T.F.T included two standout players who are originally from TC Sports Club: Hassan Thauheed "Thau", the Man of the Match in the final. Mohamed Ikram "Kuda Ikko", another key figure in the T.F.T squad. These contributions show that TC Sports Club's impact extends beyond just their own results—their influence is felt across teams and generations.

TC Sports Club may not have lifted another Baibalaa trophy in recent years, but their name remains etched in the history of the sport. Their discipline, their dedication, and the athletes they continue to produce make them a cornerstone of the Baibalaa community.

We wish TC Sports Club continued success in both Baibalaa and football. A club of champions, a club of legacy, and always a team to watch.
